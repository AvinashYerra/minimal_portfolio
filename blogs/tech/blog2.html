Other than DSA for Interviews

Date: 2025-09-10

Along with DSA, you should also prepare other important topics related to your role. These can definitely be asked during interviews.

1. Aptitude

Mostly asked in online assessments and sometimes in interviews as well.

  • Prepare from Indiabix.
  • Indiabix also has technical MCQs that are useful for Online Assessments (OA).

2. OOPS (Object-Oriented Programming)

Mostly asked for freshers, especially in SDE roles.

  • Go through definitions thoroughly and try to prepare examples for each concept.
  • Study from OOPS Sheet by Fraz (includes blog links for each topic in C++ & Java).
  • Or refer to GeeksforGeeks by searching for "OOPS concepts".

3. Databases & SQL

They might ask about normalization, DB internals, etc.

  • For detailed DBMS concepts, study Gate Smashers DBMS Playlist.
  • Take notes while studying and revise them before interviews.
  • For SQL Syntax:
    • Refer to W3Schools SQL Tutorial.
    • Solve problems from LeetCode SQL Top 50.
    • Watch Common SQL Interview Questions by Love Babbar.

4. Operating Systems

Frequently asked in interviews.

  • Usually expect definitions or basic implementations.
  • Study in-depth from Gate Smashers OS Playlist.
  • Write notes while studying.
  • Before the interview, revise using OS in One Shot by Anuj.

5. Computer Networks

Not usually very deep.

  • Go through Top 100 Networking Interview Questions.
  • Also watch this useful video → Video on Computer Networks.

6. Data Roles

Apart from the above, they may ask:

  • Aptitude, Guesstimates, Case Studies.
  • You can easily search for these online or ask similar pattern questions to ChatGPT.

7. Puzzles

Often asked in technical interviews or by HR.

  • Just search or take any puzzle question from Google and practice solving them.
  • Good source → GeeksforGeeks Puzzles.
  • Don’t try to memorize them; instead, practice how to solve such problems.

Final Tip

If you like reading, everything is available on GeeksforGeeks (GFG). You can study from there directly.

This is a complete, role-oriented preparation strategy alongside DSA.
